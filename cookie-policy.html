This Cookie Policy explains how Live in Oz ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on the Live in Oz website and related services.

This Policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.

By continuing to use our website, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this Policy, unless you disable them through your browser or our settings (where available).

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, phone) when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognise your device and remember certain information about your visit.

We also use similar technologies such as:

  • Local storage
  • Pixels (tracking pixels)
  • JavaScript-based tracking

Together, we refer to these as "cookies" in this Policy.

2. Why we use cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Make the website work properly
  • Keep you logged in and remember your preferences
  • Help you navigate between pages efficiently
  • Understand how the website is used and improve it
  • Enhance security and prevent abuse or fraud
  • (Optionally) Offer more relevant content or promotions

We do not sell the information collected by cookies.

3. Types of cookies we use

We may use the following categories of cookies:

3.1. Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as:

  • Logging in or creating an account
  • Filling out forms
  • Posting a listing or editing your profile
  • Setting your privacy or cookie preferences

Without these cookies, some parts of the website may not work correctly.

3.2. Functional / preference cookies

These cookies allow the website to:

  • Remember your preferences (language, city, layout, etc.)
  • Save certain settings so you don't have to re-enter them every time
  • Provide enhanced features and personalisation

If you disable these cookies, some features may not function as intended, but the website will still be usable.

3.3. Performance / analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website so we can improve it. For example, they may collect:

  • Which pages are visited
  • How long users stay on the site
  • Which links are clicked
  • General technical information (browser, device type, etc.)

We typically use aggregated, anonymised or pseudonymised data from these cookies. Common tools for this include analytics services (for example, Google Analytics or similar).

You can opt out of certain analytics cookies if we provide that option, or by using your browser settings or blocking tools.

3.4. Advertising / marketing cookies (if used)

If we display advertising or promote services using third parties, those partners may set cookies to:

  • Show you more relevant adverts on our site or elsewhere
  • Limit how often you see a particular advert
  • Measure the effectiveness of an advert or campaign

These cookies may track your browsing activity across different websites. You can usually manage these cookies via your browser or through the tools provided by the advertising networks.

If we are not currently using advertising cookies, this category will not be active.

4. Third-party cookies

Some cookies on our website may be set by third parties, for example:

  • Analytics providers
  • Payment processors
  • Social media platforms
  • Embedded content (for example, videos, maps or widgets)

These third parties may use their own cookies and have their own privacy and cookie policies. We do not control these cookies directly.

We recommend you review the relevant policies of these third parties for more information on how they handle your data.

5. Cookies and logged-in areas (Marketplace, rooms, jobs, Oz Chat web)

When you use logged-in areas such as the Marketplace, Accommodation, Jobs & Services, or the Oz Chat web version, we may use cookies to:

  • Maintain your session (so you stay logged in)
  • Remember your selected city or filters
  • Help you navigate between listings, messages and your account pages
  • Improve security and prevent unauthorised access

These are generally strictly necessary or functional cookies.

6. How to manage or delete cookies

You have several options to control cookies:

6.1. Browser settings

Most web browsers allow you to:

  • View which cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete individual cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Receive alerts before a cookie is stored

Please refer to your browser's help section for detailed instructions, for example:

  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Safari
  • Edge

If you block or delete cookies, some parts of the website may not function properly.

6.2. Cookie banner and preferences (if provided)

If we display a cookie banner or cookie settings panel when you first visit the website, you can use it to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Change your cookie preferences at any time

Your choices may be stored in a cookie so that we remember them when you return.

6.3. Third-party opt-outs

Some third-party services used on our website may offer their own opt-out options (for example, analytics or advertising networks). You can visit their websites for more information.

7. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" setting. At this time, we do not respond to DNT signals in a standardised way, but you can still manage cookies as explained above.
